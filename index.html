Who We Are

A Movement Built on Purpose, Not Politics

ARBR is a grassroots civic movement founded by residents of Amuwo-Odofin who believe that quality leadership, real accountability, and genuine community development are not too much to ask for.

We are not a political party. We are the people - organized, united, and determined to reshape the future of our constituency.

✓

Deepening grassroots mobilization across all wards

✓

Strengthening voter awareness and civic education

✓

Promoting leaders with competence, integrity, and measurable impact

✓

Unifying stakeholders above personal and political gain

Our Agenda

Four Pillars Driving Our Movement

Every initiative we pursue is rooted in these core commitments to the people of Amuwo-Odofin.

🔍

Accountability

We demand transparency from elected officials and hold leadership to measurable standards. Public service must be answerable to the public - always.

🏗️

Infrastructure

Paved roads, reliable power, clean water, and functional public spaces - we advocate for the basic infrastructure that every Amuwo resident deserves.

🌱

Youth Empowerment

Our young people are our greatest asset. We push for skills development, employment opportunities, and platforms that give the youth a real seat at the table.

🤝

Community Unity

Progress requires a common front. We build bridges across wards, tribes, and divides - because a united Amuwo is an unstoppable Amuwo.

Why This Matters

The Problems Are Real. The Time to Act Is Now.

Amuwo-Odofin is a constituency with immense potential - but that potential has been consistently undermined by poor governance, broken promises, and a leadership culture that serves the few over the many. That changes with us.

🛣️

Crumbling Infrastructure

Many roads in our community remain unpaved or in disrepair for years. Flood-prone areas suffer each rainy season with no lasting solution in sight.

📢

Weak Representation

Too many residents have no voice in decisions that affect their daily lives. Elected officials are often absent, inaccessible, or simply disconnected from the grassroots.

⚖️

Lack of Accountability

Budgets are not published. Projects are abandoned. Leaders face no consequence for non-performance. The culture of impunity must end.

🎓

Limited Opportunities for Youth

Young people in Amuwo-Odofin face high unemployment and limited access to skills training - despite a constituency full of energy and talent.
